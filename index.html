Chapter I

Introduction

Importance of Internship

On-the-Job Training (OJT) is an essential part of the institution’s curriculum that allows students to undergo practical training in various fields of work. It serves as a pathway for students in preparing for their future careers. OJT is a form of skill development where students learn how to perform tasks through hands-on experience. This training method exposes students to real work environments, giving them the opportunity to apply the theories and knowledge they have learned in school. It bridges the gap between academic learning and practical application. Moreover, OJT helps students acquire relevant skills, knowledge, and competencies by performing tasks in an actual workplace setting, making them more prepared and confident for their future professions..

Objectives of Internship

On-the-Job Training plays a vital role not only in educating students about their chosen careers but also in exposing them to the realities of the workplace. It allows students to experience tasks and responsibilities related to their field of study, giving them a clearer understanding of professional expectations.The internship serves as a stepping stone for students to gain valuable knowledge and practical experience that will benefit them in their future careers. It helps develop their competence and enables them to perform tasks effectively and efficiently. Additionally, OJT enhances students’ professionalism by teaching them how to interact and communicate with different types of people in the workplace.Through this training, students learn how to handle real-life situations, solve problems, and work collaboratively with colleagues. By the end of the internship, they are expected to become more confident, responsible, and socially adaptable individuals..

About the College

Occidental Mindoro State College in the only state college in the province of Occidental Mindoro located in the heart of them municipality of San Jose, which is a stone’s throw away from the Municipal Hall and the St. Joseph, The Work Cathedral. The town on the other hand, is located 130 kilometers southeast of the Provincial Capital and in approximately 230 nautical miles from the National Capital Region.

The College had its humble beginning as a Barrio High School in 1966 patterned after Dr. Pedro Orata’s Barangay High School, an idea conceived by San Jose Pilot Elementary School PTA President and also town Mayor Juan G. Santos, Sr. and worked out by the Schools Division Superintendent, Mr. Sofronio Fadre and East District Supervisor, Mr. Mariano Ramirez.Mayor Tirso Abeleda in 1967approved Resolution No. 23 adopting and constituting the self-supporting public secondary school as the San Jose Municipal High School.

The late Congressman Pedro C. Medalla, Sr. authored R.A. No. 6568, which elevated San Jose Municipal High School into San Jose National High School (SJNHS). Mr. Bernabe Macaraig was appointed Principal. Enrollment grew significantly during that time, reaching up to 3,000.

On June 24, 1986, by the virtue of Batas Pambansa Blg. 531, sponsored by the late Congressman Pedro T. Mendiola, Sr. SJNHS was elevated into Occidental Mindoro State College with Mr. Bernabe Macaraig as its first Superintendent.

On April 1, 1886, Ministry of Educational Culture and Sports (MECS) Minister, Lourdes R. Quisumbing appointed Mrs. Virginia A. Sicat, then Assistant Principal, as Officer-In-Charge of OMSC. On the same year, Dr. Ofelia A. Rebong was installed as the first President of the College. It was during her seven years’ term as College President that staff development was given due consideration.

In the school year 1991-1992, the College absorbed the Occidental Mindoro Community College, Mamburao as a satellite campus offering baccalaureate and non-degree courses. It had likewise acquired the 98.8725-hectare lot from the Organization for the International Scientific and Cultural Advancement Council (OISCA) through the Provincial Board and Provincial Development Council. The satellite campus became known as the OMSC-Mamburao Campus.

After the retirement of Dr. Rebong, the then Vice President for Academic Affairs and Dean of the Graduate School, Dr. Sofronio S. Sanqui, was appointed College President on December 19, 1994 and sworn into office on December 29, of the same year by DECS Secretary Ricardo T. Gloria.

Focusing on human resource development and student’s welfare, Dr. Sanqui’s management priorities brought about significant shifts in the social, cultural and economic structure of the College.

OMSC has grown tremendously and has opened up four (4) service areas namely: the Damayan Center constructed in a 2-hectare land donated by Mr. Romeo Lumbo in San Isidro, San Jose, Occidental Mindoro; OMSC-Mamburao; the Experimental Forestry - 28.4974 – hectare land adjacent to the Damayan Center; the Caminawit Extension Program in Caminawit, San Jose, Occidental Mindoro and the Agro-Forestry Center which is now fully developed located at a 8.7 hectare land at Brgy. Labangan, San Jose, Occidental Mindoro.

Internship Analysis Report

Name: Jericho C. Batolio Date: May 5, 2025
Internship Site: Multimedia
1. Setting
During my internship, I was actively engaged in both administrative and technical support tasks, including data encoding, document filing, scheduling, and assisting in user intake as well as record updates. I chose to immerse myself in these responsibilities to gain hands-on experience and further enhance my practical skills through real-world exposure.
Upon my initial observation, I found that the organization maintained a well-structured and task-oriented environment. The workplace emphasized accuracy, accountability, and collaboration among team members. The staff were approachable, supportive, and cooperative, which contributed to a smooth workflow and efficient handling of technical concerns. Additionally, there was a strong emphasis on maintaining the functionality of equipment and ensuring that users had a productive and satisfactory experience.
2. Site Supervisor
My site supervisor exhibited excellent technical expertise and effective leadership qualities. They provided clear instructions, guided me through various troubleshooting procedures, and offered constructive feedback to help me improve. Their willingness to share knowledge enabled me to better understand both fundamental and more advanced technical tasks.
Through their guidance and mentorship, I gained confidence in managing computer systems, assisting users, and resolving technical issues. I truly appreciated their patience, support, and encouragement, which motivated me to continuously enhance my skills. Furthermore, I also learned a great deal from other staff members who contributed to my growth by teaching me efficient and professional ways of completing tasks.
3. Environmental Conditions or Events
Throughout the duration of my internship, I did not encounter any significant problems or major challenges. The work setting remained stable, organized, and conducive to learning. The facilities and equipment were generally well-maintained, and the daily workflow allowed me to complete assigned tasks effectively.
Although minor adjustments were sometimes needed due to workload changes, these situations helped me become more adaptable and resourceful. Overall, the environment provided a positive and practical training ground where I was able to grow both technically and professionally.
4. Self-Assessment
This internship significantly improved my technical, interpersonal, and professional skills. I became more confident in handling computer-related tasks, assisting users, and applying troubleshooting techniques. I also improved my communication skills by interacting with supervisors, staff, and students in a professional manner.
I learned the value of discipline, punctuality, and responsibility in the workplace. I also developed better time management and adaptability, especially when handling multiple tasks. Overall, I can say that I have grown into a more competent and career-ready individual through this internship experience.
5. General
In general, my internship experience was meaningful, productive, and highly beneficial to my development as a BSIT student. It allowed me to connect classroom learning with actual workplace practices, giving me a deeper understanding of my chosen field.
The internship not only strengthened my technical foundation but also shaped my work attitude and professional values. I am grateful for the opportunity to learn from experienced personnel and to contribute through the tasks entrusted to me. This experience has prepared me to face future career challenges with greater confidence and competence.
